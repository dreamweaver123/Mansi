Design

By : Tim J. 3 Problems X 3 Designers South Square

Economic Design : Simplicity allows for the cultural texture to emerge.

A Multicultural Emphasis : Billboards for local merchants encourage commerce.

In the 1930s, South Square was a bustling agora. Families of various ethnic origins that migrated to this burgeoning metropolis would set up booths to sell food, clothing, jewelry, and anything else they could produce to stay afloat. In 1962 Meridien built two elevated highways that sealed off the area from the rest of the city and all but doomed the square to abandonment and squalor. Now, Meridien has almost finished demolishing the highways and is hoping to revitalize the area.

Local asked three of Meridien's top creatives to imagine what South Square could become in light of this urban renewal.

Opal, a partner in the design consortium Dodecahedron, which specializes in urban renewal and sustainable design:

"South Square is one of the first areas in Meridien that was truly multicultural and thrived on the interrelationship between ethnic groups. My proposal is to bring back the marketplace aura of the 1930s and 1940s by establishing a retail market agora with only local merchants. Billboards would be dedicated solely to these local merchants as well as used as showcases for local artists' work."
